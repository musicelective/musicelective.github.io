INTRODUCTION TO PROGRAM

See what Springfield Music course has to offer to their students, according to Ms. Ruth.

What do students learn? 

Students will learn about musicianship, the arrangements of music, the theory of music, as well as simply appreciating the art of music.


Projects in Semester 1?

Students will learn about the musical arrangements. For the 10th graders, this year they had to compose an idea of a performance to fit with the theme of "dream to believe, believe to become" for the 20th Anniversary celebration. In the end, they settled with a tribute to Beyonce, as it the 20th anniversary for her musical career, too. The students had to make their own choreographies, as assigned to each teams, to unite as a medley. 


What will the students understand to do by the end of the year?

They will understand that music can be used in a lot of areas in life.


What are the concepts learned?

By this year, the students will learn about song arrangements and apply it into a group, or a band, as they had to cover a song in groups of three, as long as there is at least one student on the instruments and one student on the vocals. Aside from learning how to play an instrument/vocalising on a song, they will also learn how to cooperate in a team by practicing, for cooperation in a band is an essential. Students will have to depend on each other to make a song sound good. 


What do you expect the students to achieve by joining your course?

Simple. My only expectation for them is to be able to enjoy music.
